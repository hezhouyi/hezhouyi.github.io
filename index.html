About me

I am an M.Phil student at the Department of Chemistry, Hong Kong University of Science and Technology supervised by Prof. Haibin Su. Currently, I am working on Theoretical Biophysics and Computational Biology.

I proudly received my B.Sc. degree in Chemical Physics from University of Science and Technology of China (USTC), which is considered one of the best Universities in the fields of frontier research and undergraduate education in China. Publication related to my undergraduate thesis can be seen in Unit Cell Consistency of Maximally Localized Wannier Functions.

During my M.Phil study, my research especially focuses on the application of statistical mechanics and evolutionary theory in complex biological systems such as CRISPR Cas9 systems and nucleotide/protein sequence space. Preprint of my recent work can be seen in Dynamic Expedition of Leading Mutations in SARS-CoV-2 Spike Glycoproteins.

I am interested in interdisciplinary research and currently looking for a Ph.D. position!
